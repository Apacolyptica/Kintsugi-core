Risk brainstorming (not exactly IEEE format, but it's a prototype)
Risk Likelyhood Impact (worst case) Contingency
Member leaves project: Low Severe Other members take over lost member's work, ensure each member has a working competence of all duties. Recalculate WBS and schedule accordingly.
Member Gets Sick (mild) Medium Low-Medium If significant impact, readjust accordingly. PM can provide emergency manpower for the sick person (it's preferred they recover and work competently rather than have extra errors under the weather)
Member Gets Sick (severe) Low-Medium High See "member leaves project." Will re-re calculate things once member recovers enough to competently perform his/her duties.
Member gets injured (debilitating)Low Severe Assuming the injury prevents competent performance of his/her duties, see the compensation for "member leaves project."
Member has family emergency Low Medium-High Assuming family emergency forbids work, other members will take over duties. If a significant time is needed for the lost member, adjust WBS and schedule accordingly.
Member has to leave town (short time)Medium Medium Try to find a means to work remotely. Adjust WBS and schedule if this proves impossible.
Member has to leave town (long time)Low High Try to find a means to do work remotely. Recalculate according to new schedule. See "member leaves project" if the situation cannot be salvaged.
Member's computer crashes Medium Medium-High Keep files in Github for file sharing
Project Falls Behind Schedule Medium-High(sadly) Medium-High Recalculate WBS, PM will provide emergency manpower to whatever areas are highest priority.
Member's internet connection failsMedium-High Medium-High Find a different means of connection, work off line if possible (alert other members via text or phone e-mail or social network). If the problem persists, try to find a dedicated school workspace with a strong internet connection.
Members lack key knowledge Very High Medium-High Adjust schedule estiamtes to assume research is required, place that into WBS to indicate a valid task and duty (if proof of progress is needed, a mini-report of "what I researched" can be written).
Members non-project schedule is too heavyVery High High See "project falls behind schedule."
Customer is not available High Medium Make judgments as the team sees fit, leave customer e-mail indicating judgments to the customer so they can confirm/deny them when time permits.
Report is behind schedule Medium High If possible reallocate manpower to the report (however, the physical project still gets priority). PM will write it if necessary.
Testing is behind schedule Medium-High Medium-High Reallocate manpower for testing if possible. If problem persists, reorganize testing plan.
Documentation is behind scheduleMedium-High Medium-High Reallocate manpower for documentation if possible. Try to make "prototype documents" to work around for when work-light periods occur (to use as a base for catching up).
Schedule estimation is significantly offHigh High Readjust schedule estimation accordingly. Possibly readjust expecations to core priorities. Let customer know.
Project requirements are unclearVery High High Ask customer CONCRETE questions to pierce any ambiguity "pierce the fluffy" as my contact at Aker called it).
Project diverges from customers visionMedium High Adjust project accordingly, salvage what we can (even unused parts can be good code samples to cannibalize from on present or future projects, so no deleting unless we have to).
Tasks are too complex High Medium Further decompose WBS. Divide accordingly. Chances are, it entails work we are already doing anyway on some level, so we just need to more clearly divide it.
Scope Creep High Severe Negotiate very clear system boundaries, make sure core priorities are established. Go out of the way to ask "what WON'T this application do in terms of the larger system."
Miscommunication between customer and teamVery-High High Try to maintain clear communication (I know it's a "well duh" answer, but still). Find a means to get concrete confirmation that we are talking about the same thing.
